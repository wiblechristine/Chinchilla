Chinchilla Lanigera

An Unusual Rodent

The long-tailed variant of the Chinchilla genus, C. lanigera are herd herbivores that dwell in the Chilean Andes, among several other coastal countries in South America. They are considered endangered after historically being farmed for their fur. The domestic chinchilla known in the US is believed to be descendant from C. lanigera, after the initial batch was introduced in 1923 with permission of the Chilean government. Their plush fur is unable to air dry so they mustn't get wet, for they will develop 'fur rot', a fungus. In the wild chins bathe in volcanic ash; for a house chin, finely ground pumice stone is the exclusive solution (seen here). If you seek a temperamental companion, you will find most commonly the standard grey variety, but domestic chins also come in beige, white mosaic, black velvet, violet, and many others.
